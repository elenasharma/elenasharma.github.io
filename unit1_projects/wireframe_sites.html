wireframing

YouTube

YouTube facilitates its users' searches for videos while allowing them to save data for a customizable interface and promoting sponsored videos/channels. I chose to look at my homepage when I'm signed in with "My Subscriptions" selected (since users can browse YouTube without signing in) to showcase the visual challenge of balancing user subscriptions and settings with sponsored "recommended blogs." Another visual challenge is to provide a large amount of content and navigability without being too busy.

Tumblr

Tumblr is even more of a user-driven experience than YouTube, since the dashboard - the primary feature and focus - is entirely created by the postings of the blogs that the user follows. Tumblr solves the problem of bringing different types of media together on a quickly updating blogging platform with its unending dashboard. The visual challenge is to keep the focus on the dashboard, but not neglecting the other settings (such as managing account/blog and adding media).

Wikipedia

Wikipedia's task is to present information in an efficient and user-friendly manner. The visual challenge here is to not make the pages too text-heavy, which would lose some readers' interest/attention spans - some ways that Wikipedia tackles this challenge include breaking its articles up into sections and including a table of contents on the left and a quick info box on the upper right of articles.
